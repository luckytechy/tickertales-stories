A Garage Asked for a Tyre Pump. The Answer Built India's Most Ambitious Global Industrial Empire.

They started making car lifts for petrol stations. Then a mechanic asked for something small to inflate tyres. That single request became a ₹3,500 crore global compressor company targeting to be No. 2 in the world.

Chapter One

The Company Hidden Inside Every Factory, Hospital, and Railway Station

Dr. Jairam Varadaraj

Managing Director · Third Generation · Elgi Equipments

MBA and PhD from US universities, Dr. Jairam returned to Coimbatore not to coast on a family legacy, but to take on Atlas Copco, Ingersoll Rand, and Kaeser on their home turf. The ambition: take Elgi from No. 8 to No. 2 in the world.

Walk into a hospital and breathe the clean air in the ICU. The compressor keeping that air oil-free might be made in Coimbatore. Board a train on Indian Railways. The compressed air system keeping the brakes and doors working might be made in Coimbatore. Step into any pharmaceutical factory and watch the sterile packaging lines run. The compressor might be made in Coimbatore.

The company is Elgi Equipments. Outside of industrial procurement circles, almost nobody knows the name. Inside those circles, the name has been earning its reputation for over six decades — one compressor sale at a time, in 120 countries.

It began not with a grand plan but with a mechanic's practical request. In 1960, Elgi was making car lifts and grease pumps for petrol stations in Coimbatore. Garages kept asking: can you make something small to inflate tyres? The founders made one. Then another. Then better versions for bigger machines. By the time they realised what they had, the compressor had become the entire company.

⚡ Name Trivia

The name ELGI is not a word — it is the initials of the company's original founder, L.G. Varadarajulu. Every letter in the brand name is a piece of family legacy, carried forward now into its third generation of leadership.

Chapter Two

The Audacity of Competing Against Atlas Copco

Atlas Copco was founded in 1873. Ingersoll Rand in 1871. Kaeser in 1919. These are companies with over a century of global brand trust, deep distribution networks, and engineering reputations built across two world wars and multiple industrial revolutions.

Elgi, a third-generation Coimbatore family business, decided it wanted to beat them.

The strategy Dr. Jairam Varadaraj chose was not to fight them in their strongest markets on their strongest terms. It was to leverage India's structural manufacturing advantage: world-class engineering at Indian cost structures. A compressor built in Coimbatore with the same reliability as one built in Stuttgart, priced competitively enough to win customers in Asia, Australia, and now Europe.

This required investment that looked irrational from the outside. A 100-acre greenfield manufacturing plant in Coimbatore. Acquisitions in Italy and Australia to build local credibility and distribution. Direct sales teams in dozens of countries. R&D spend to develop oil-free compressors for pharmaceutical lines and electric compressors for the EV transition — both high-value product categories where incumbents charge premium prices.

“We are not building a good Indian compressor company. We are building one of the best compressor companies in the world.”

— Dr. Jairam Varadaraj, Managing Director, Elgi Equipments
The Journey

Six Decades from Petrol Station Lifts to 120 Countries

1960
Founded in Coimbatore — car lifts and grease pumps
L.G. Varadarajulu incorporates Elgi Equipments in Coimbatore, initially serving petrol stations with car lifts and lubrication equipment. The air compressor comes almost by accident — a response to what mechanics kept asking for.
1962
First air compressor — the product that changed everything
Elgi builds its first air compressor, responding to requests from garages wanting to inflate tyres. The product works better than expected. The founders begin to see that industrial compressed air is a much larger opportunity than petrol station equipment.
1980s–90s
Building India's industrial customer base
Over two decades, Elgi builds relationships with Indian manufacturing, railways, and healthcare — the reliable domestic foundation that funds everything that comes next. Indian Railways becomes a key customer for compressed air braking and door systems.
Early 2000s
Dr. Jairam Varadaraj sets the global ambition
Third-generation leader Dr. Jairam Varadaraj takes the helm with a clear declaration: Elgi will not be content being the best Indian compressor maker. The target is to be one of the top two compressor companies in the world.
2010s
Acquisitions in Italy and Australia; 100-acre greenfield plant
Elgi acquires Patton's (Australia) and a European manufacturing facility in Italy, establishing local credibility in markets where India-origin was a barrier. A 100-acre greenfield plant in Coimbatore gives Elgi the manufacturing scale to compete globally on cost without compromising quality.
FY2025
₹3,510 Cr revenue · 120 countries · No. 8 globally — targeting No. 2
Elgi reaches ₹3,510 crore in revenue with a direct presence in 120 countries, ranking No. 8 globally in air compressors. The oil-free range serves pharma and food manufacturing. The electric compressor range positions Elgi for the EV manufacturing cycle.

The Takeaway

What Elgi Teaches Every Investor

🌎
The most ambitious goals are not announced loudly. They are built quietly, one country at a time.

Elgi Equipments has been building global reach since before most Indian companies had a passport strategy. No one told a Coimbatore family business to take on Atlas Copco. They simply decided that being the best compressor company in India was not enough — and started doing the work that requires.

That work involves acquisitions in Italy, plants in Coimbatore, engineers in Australia, and oil-free compressors for pharma lines in Europe. It is unglamorous, capital-intensive, and multi-decade. Which is exactly why nobody else is doing it at the same scale — and exactly why the moat is widening every year.
