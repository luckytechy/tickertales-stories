The Beginning

Where It All Started

In 1969, two public-sector engineers in Hyderabad made a quiet, audacious bet: that a private company could make the most precise, mission-critical parts in India — components for nuclear reactors and space rockets. P. Ravindra Reddy and K. Satyanarayana Reddy started MTAR in 1970 with zero precedent. Their first contract: coolant channel assemblies for nuclear reactor cores. ISRO and NPCIL became clients. Over 30 years later, MTAR parts have flown on GSLV and PSLV rockets. Nobody's heard of them. The rockets couldn't launch without them.
