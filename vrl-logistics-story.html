The Beginning

Where It All Started

In 1976, Vijay Sankeshwar borrowed Rs 2 lakhs in Gadag — a small town in North Karnataka — and bought one truck. He had no logistics training, no technology, no GPS, no mobile phone. When the truck left with a cargo delivery, he had no way to contact the driver or track the shipment. He did not know if the delivery had reached the destination until the truck physically returned to Gadag. He suffered accidents, cargo losses, and financial setbacks that would have broken most people. He kept going — one truck at a time. In 1978 he moved to Hubballi and bought three more trucks. By 1990 he had 117 vehicles and Rs 4 Cr+ turnover. Today VRL Logistics has 5,745 commercial vehicles and is the largest fleet owner in India. The journey from one borrowed truck to India’s biggest fleet took 40 years of grinding, unglamorous, relentless work.

What They Were Up Against

Operating a logistics business in the pre-mobile, pre-GPS era with no way to track vehicles once they left; severe losses from road accidents and cargo damage; building a reliable network in an era of terrible Indian road infrastructure; establishing trust with customers when delivery timelines were entirely unpredictable

The turning point came when 1983: formally incorporating Vijayanand Roadlines Limited and systematising operations; the shift from running individual trucks to building a structured hub-and-spoke network that could scale across Karnataka and eventually all of India
