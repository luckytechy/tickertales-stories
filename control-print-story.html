The Beginning

Where It All Started

Every product you buy has a date printed on it. That date is stamped at the factory by an industrial coding and marking machine working at up to 80,000 decisions per second. In 1991, Shiva Kabra started Control Print to supply these machines to Indian FMCG, pharma, and food companies — competing against entrenched global giants like Domino (UK) and Videojet (USA). Today Control Print has 60,000+ installed machines across India running inside Amul's dairy plants, pharma packaging lines, and biscuit factories. The machines are almost free. The inks, solvents, and laser consumables generate recurring revenue year after year.

The Business

What the Model Actually Is

Razor-and-blade model in B2B industrial equipment — machines are sold at low margin but generate high-margin recurring consumables revenue year after year; once a machine is installed on a live production line, switching it out means line stoppage, operator retraining, and re-qualification — enormous switching costs that create a sticky annuity revenue stream

FMCG companies investing in automated production lines; pharma serialization and track-and-trace regulations mandating advanced coding; Control Print expanding into laser coding which is a structural upgrade cycle
